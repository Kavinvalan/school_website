Academics
Examination Results

A tradition of island-level rankings

In 1981, K. Ramakrishnan topped the G.C.E. Advanced Level with 360 marks; the following year three students — including a set of twins — scored 4 distinctions, a feat achieved by no other school in the island that year. The tradition continues: S. Thuvarakan took the 1st island rank in Physical Science in 2017, and Kanthathasan Thasarath the 2nd in 2025.

Between 1986 and 1994 alone, more than a dozen Hartleyites won scholarships to study Medicine and Engineering in Australia after scoring 3A or 4A passes — a run that placed the college among the most competitive Tamil-medium schools on the island.

  • 360 marks — Island-topping A/L score, K. Ramakrishnan, 1981
  • 2nd, National Level — A.C. John Niraj, Mathematics, 2009; competed at the Asian Mathematical Olympiad, Taiwan
  • 1st & 2nd Island Ranks — Physical Science, G.C.E. A/L, 2017 & 2025
Athletics
On The Field

A discus-throwing dynasty

Hartley's throwers have carried national colours for two decades. Vadiveswaran Harikaran won Colours Awards in discus at Under-17, Under-19 and Under-21 level between 2007 and 2010; T. Abishanth and B. Ananth took gold and silver in the 2016 Junior National Championship; S. Mithunraj set a national U-15 discus record in 2017 and later took three golds with a national hammer-throw record at the 2022 Junior Nationals.

The college has also produced national boxing champions — S. Jivanth and S. Kiritharan both took 1st place at the Sri Lanka Schools Boxing Association nationals in 2015 — alongside pole vault, chess and a cricket programme dating to 1930.

Innovation & Culture
Beyond The Classroom

From Tamil Day to the Maths Olympiad

Hartley's choirs and orators have won 1st place at the National Level Tamil Day competition in multiple years running, while Mahendran Saivatharsan took gold at the National Young Inventors Competition in 2014. In 2016, S. Jathusan represented Sri Lanka at the International Mathematical Olympiad after winning through the national round.

Today that spirit continues through clubs such as Aerowings and regular wins at the Yarl Geek Challenge robotics and innovation competition.

  • Multiple 1st places — National Level Tamil Day, group & solo song
  • Gold Medal — National Young Inventors Competition, 2014
  • International Level — Mathematical Olympiad, 2016
School Spirit

The four houses

Every Hartleyite belongs to one of four houses, named for principals who shaped the college — competing each year at the Inter-House Athletic Meet.

Thamotharam

Named for Mr. C.P. Thamotharam, principal 1915–1943, who oversaw the college's renaming to Hartley in 1916. Athletic Meet champions in 2019, 2020, 2023, 2024 and 2025.

Sherrad

Named for Mr. J.C.T. Sherrard, whose 31-year tenure from 1875 made the school so well known it was called "Sherrad's School." Runners-up in 2020 and 2024/25.

Kanapathippillai

Named for Mr. S. Kanapathipillai, principal 1907–1912, who briefly led the college after Mr. Paulpillai's early passing. First runners-up in 2018 and 2020.

Paulpillai

Named for Mr. S.A. Paulpillai, principal in 1906, honouring the college's earliest years of academic ambition. Champions of the 2018 Inter-House Athletic Meet.

A Living Chronicle

186 years, in milestones

Compiled from the college's own historical notes, kept by former Deputy Principal Mr. N. Gunaseelan and staff member Mrs. B. Linashkumar.

1838

Founded as Wesleyan Mission Central School

Established by Rev. Dr. Peter Percival — scholar, educationist, and compiler of the English–Tamil dictionary, who also translated the Bible into Tamil.

1875

Move to the present site

The school relocated to the grounds it occupies today; the old site became a girls' boarding school, now the Methodist Girls' High School.

1916

Renamed Hartley College

Rev. Marshall Hartley, Secretary of the Methodist Mission for the East, laid the foundation for a science laboratory, and the school took his name.

1930

The hostel & cricket

The hostel building was constructed and cricket was formally introduced to the college.

1953

Centenary celebrated

The college marked over a century of continuous service to the students of the region.

1984

The library fire

The college library, holding 6,690 books, was destroyed by fire along with several classrooms.

1985–1990

Displacement and return

The school operated from a village 3km away at Puttalai before the entire campus was shifted back to its original site in 1990.

1989

150th anniversary

A commemorative postage stamp and first-day cover were issued to mark the sesquicentennial.

2012

Website launched

The official Hartley College website was declared open, alongside new infrastructure including an inner road, canteen and water tank funded by international donors.

2013

175th anniversary

Four days of exhibitions, tournaments and a magazine release, marked with a second commemorative postage stamp.

2014

Iyan Karan Hostel opens

Old boy and German senator Ian Karan funded a new hostel, opened in the presence of the Chief Minister of the Northern Province. Mr. T. Muhunthan took over as principal the same year.

2015

Technology Lab & a Prime Ministerial visit

The Technology Lab opened, and Prime Minister Hon. Ranil Wickremesinghe paid an official visit to the school on 27th March.

2024

Dr. V.K. Samuel Laboratory opens

A three-storey science laboratory, funded by Ian Karan in memory of his brother, opened with dedicated floors for Chemistry, Physics and Biology.

2025

New leadership

Mr. V. Uthayamohan assumed duties as principal, continuing a line of leadership recorded since 1851.

Source: Hartley College Historical Notes, hartley.lk
