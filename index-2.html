Why We're Here

Vision, mission & motto

Three commitments have guided the college since 1838 — to its students, its staff, and the town of Point Pedro.

01 — VISION

Leading through excellence

To be the leading school in Sri Lanka, serving all pupils through our strength and commitment to academic excellence.

02 — MISSION

Comprehensive & competitive

A comprehensive, competitive and innovative education, delivered by a friendly and motivated staff — for students, teachers, and the wider community alike.

03 — MOTTO

"Fiat Lux"

Let there be light — a charge to illuminate every pupil who passes through these gates, given by our founders in 1838.

Academics
Examination Results

Consistently among the island's best

Hartley has repeatedly placed students at the very top of the national G.C.E. Advanced Level rankings — S. Thuvarakan took the 1st Rank Island-wide in Physical Science (2017), and Kanthathasan Thasarath secured 2nd island rank in the same stream in 2025.

Dozens of Hartleyites enter the Engineering and Medical faculties each year, several with full scholarships to study abroad in Australia and beyond.

  • 1st Island Rank — Physical Science, G.C.E. A/L 2017
  • 2nd Island Rank — Physical Science, G.C.E. A/L 2025
  • 3rd Tamil-medium school — G.C.E. O/L performance, island-wide (2011)
Athletics
On The Field

Record-breaking on the track

Hartley's throwers have long dominated national athletics. S. Mithunraj set a national U-15 discus record (53.23m) in 2017 and later took three gold medals with a national hammer-throw record at the 2022 Junior Nationals.

The college also fields nationally ranked boxers, chess players and a cricket programme with roots stretching back to 1930.

School Spirit

The four houses

Every Hartleyite belongs to one of four houses, named for principals who shaped the college — competing each year at the Inter-House Athletic Meet.

Thamotharam

Named for Mr. C.P. Thamotharam, principal from 1915–1943, who oversaw the college's renaming to Hartley in 1916.

Sherrad

Named for Mr. J.C.T. Sherrard, whose 31-year tenure from 1875 made the school so well known it was called "Sherrad's School."

Kanapathippillai

Named for Mr. S. Kanapathipillai, who led the college in the early 20th century during a period of steady growth.

Paulpillai

Named for Mr. S.A. Paulpillai, principal in 1906, honouring the college's earliest years of academic ambition.

A Living Chronicle

186 years, in milestones

From a mission school on the peninsula to a national name in academics, sport and the arts.

1838

Founded as Wesleyan Mission Central School

Established by Rev. Dr. Peter Percival — scholar, educationist, and compiler of the English–Tamil dictionary and Tamil Bible translation.

1875

Move to the present site

The school relocated to the grounds it occupies today in Point Pedro; the old site became the Methodist Girls' High School.

1916

Renamed Hartley College

Rev. Marshall Hartley, Secretary of the Methodist Mission for the East, laid the foundation for a science laboratory, and the school took his name.

1930

Cricket and the hostel

The hostel building was constructed and cricket was formally introduced to the college.

1953

Centenary celebrated

The college marked over a century of continuous service to the region's students.

1984 – 1990

Displacement and return

The library was destroyed by fire and the school was displaced during the conflict, before returning fully to its original site in 1990.

2013

175th anniversary

Four days of exhibitions, tournaments and a magazine release, marked with a special commemorative postage stamp.

2014 – 2015

Iyan Karan Hostel & Technology Lab

Old boy and German senator Ian Karan funded a new hostel and technology laboratory, opened with the Chief Minister of the Northern Province in attendance.

2023

185th anniversary

Marked with a sponsored walk, tree planting, and cricket matches among old boys and staff.

2024

Dr. V.K. Samuel Laboratory opens

A three-storey science laboratory, funded by Ian Karan in memory of his brother, opened with dedicated floors for Chemistry, Physics and Biology.

2025

21st Principal appointed

Mr. V. Uthayamohan assumed duties as principal, continuing an unbroken line of leadership stretching back to 1851.
